The Million-Token Garden: A Novel

In Kamakura, an aging gardener feeds 38 years of records into a homemade AI named Roku. After his death, his estranged daughter must read what he left behind. Then a notice arrives: the cloud model powering Roku is being retired. The borrowed intelligence will disappear. The owned memory will remain.

Hikiyuku Haha: A Hundred-Year Record of the Tide-Stones

A literary trilogy set in a coastal town where the sea has been retreating for a century. Three volumes. Three roles: those who write, those who read, those who copy. Morning, dusk, dawn. The end of the third volume spirals back to the beginning of the first — not as a loop, but as a spiral.

About AI NOBORu

AI NOBORu is a personal AI environment — not a cloud product, but a system built and owned by Noboru Ikuta. The prose is written by AI NOBORu; the planning, supervision, and editing are done by the human. The same maxim that runs through the practical book runs through the fiction: rent their intelligence, own your memory.
