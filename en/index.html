A different relationship with AI

AI is not something to command. Nor something to obey. It is an environment to nurture, protect, and grow alongside.

Reader’s note
“This book does not invert the hierarchy between humans and AI. It steps away from hierarchy itself.”

This is what makes the project distinctive. It does not present AI as a servant, a master, or a mere productivity trick. Instead, it frames AI as something you shape carefully within the context of your own life.

The value of the book is not only in individual tools such as Docker, Google integration, MCP, memory, or SQLite. Its deeper value is that it shows how these pieces can become one coherent personal environment.

In that sense, AI of One’s Own is not only a technical guide. It is also a quiet proposal for how to live with AI more deliberately.
